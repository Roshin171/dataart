1900

Edwardian Era Elegance

The early 1900s fashion featured S-shaped corsets, high collars, and lavish gowns that emphasized formality and femininity.

1910

Poiret and the Birth of Modernism

Designer Paul Poiret rejected corsets, introducing free-flowing silhouettes and bold colors that shaped early modern fashion.

1920

The Flapper Revolution

The roaring twenties introduced short hemlines, fringe, beads, and bobbed hair. Fashion reflected women's liberation and nightlife culture.

1930

Hollywood Glamour

Fashion in the 1930s turned to bias-cut gowns, satin fabrics, and long lines inspired by silver screen stars like Jean Harlow and Marlene Dietrich.

1940

Utility and Wartime Fashion

World War II led to rationing, resulting in simple, structured silhouettes, shorter skirts, and functional fashion for women in the workforce.

1977

Rise of Punk & Street Fashion

The punk movement brought rebellion to the runway — safety pins, ripped clothes, leather, and Vivienne Westwood defined the 1977 street style.

It challenged traditional fashion norms and inspired a generation of DIY expression.
